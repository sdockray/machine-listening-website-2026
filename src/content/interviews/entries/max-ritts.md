We talk with [Max](https://www.clarku.edu/faculty/profiles/max-ritts/) about the politics of [‘smart oceans governance’](https://rgs-ibg.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/tran.12586) and the growing application of machine listening to [‘conservation acoustics’.](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0016718521001214) In the process, we also cover his amazing work on [‘military cetology’](https://read.dukeupress.edu/environmental-humanities/article/8/2/196/8119/Military-Cetology) and the [social construction of whale song](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/0263775817711706), among other things. All this is going to feature in Max’s forthcoming book *A Resonant Ecology* (Duke UP). If you like this conversation, you might also like [Smart Forest Radio](https://atlas.smartforests.net/en/radio/), a podcast Max has been involved with, which includes many episodes on digital bioacoustics.
