[Bernard Mont-Reynaud](https://www.linkedin.com/in/bernard-mont-reynaud-b082/) is a computer scientist who worked on and around machine listening for more than 50 years before his retirement in 2023. After a PhD at Stanford in the 1970s, he moved to Berkeley, before returning to Stanford’s CCRMA to pursue work on source separation and music. After time at Sony, Audience and several other companies, Mont-Reynaud became Principal Scientist at [SoundHound](https://www.soundhound.com) in 2010. We speak to him about his long career in machine listening, including his involvement with the histories of computer music and music intelligence, source separation, auditory scene analysis, and voice assistants.
